Combat !!

When push comes to shove things get serious quickly. This game has no to hit roll, so somebody is taking damage each turn. You simply state your target, roll your weapon die, and the target takes that much damage. That applies for the enemies on their turn as well!
Some things of note:

  • Initiative is side based, so all allies attack, then all enemies attack. This varies round after round so you (or enemies) may get two turns in a row.
  • Movement is 'zone' based, an abstract area that relates to other areas. A living room is one zone, while the grand staircase is another, while the upstairs balcony is a third.
    You can travel to an adjacent zone and do something, or move to a faraway zone, if your movement is not blocked.
  • Stacking 5 attackers on one target doesn't work. Only one damage die result applies (the highest).
    • Get creative to offset this. Use tactics. Grabbing and pinning an enemy down may grant Enhanced damage for the turn.
  • Actions occur in roughly this order: Missiles, Movement, Melee, Magic. With Initiative winners acting first, and losers following.
    • If you lose initiative and take Ability damage on your turn, you will lose any ranged attack, or any spell cast.

Combat Structure

If one side is unaware of an incoming attack, the damage goes straight off of the target's Strength score, hp does not apply. Once both sides are aware of the conflict:

  1. I'll describe the scene around you, and recap the last few seconds. We'll reference a whiteboard if we need it for positioning ourselves in the zones.
  2. I'll describe the enemies' intent for the upcoming round. Basically what will happen if you do nothing.
  3. You'll describe what you want to do this round. Who you are attacking, what weapon, what spells.
    • I need to know your melee and ranged targets. If you are casting a spell, and how many Magic Die you are using.
    • Alternatively, I need to know if you are doing a special maneuver this round!
  4. One player will roll a d6 for initiative: 4-6 wins initiative for the round.
  5. I'll describe the actions of the initiative winners calling for rolls as they are needed, then follow suit with the losers.
    • When you lose initiative, you run the risk of losing an action !!
        • If you are attacking at range, or casting a spell and you take Ability score damage: you lose the attack / spell, but still expend the resource (arrow or M.D.). This applies to enemies as well!
    • If your chosen action is impossible to perform after losing initiative, you can adjust your course of action slightly.
  6. If the enemies have taken heavy casualties, I may have the leader make a Charisma save to see if the group wants to continue fighting. Most creatures would rather flee than fight to the death.
    • If they decide to continue to battle, we will return to step 1. Otherwise I'll state their intent to flee the next round.

Notes:

Out of all enemies attacking one target, only the highest damage die applies for the round.
When attempting maneuvers in combat, the side most at risk makes a save to prevent ill effects.
Pushing for advantages with maneuvers can result in the Enhanced or Impaired status!!

Crits:

Whenever 2 or more dice are rolled against a singular target, and at least two dice roll maximum results, you've scored a critical hit!

  • Double the damage.
  • When the target rolls to prevent Critical Damage, the roll is made at +4.

Zones:

Try to break the area up into 2-4 zones, bonus points for making something intractable at each zone that changes the battlefield in some way (I broke the statue, now there are glass shards everywhere!).

Mass Combat

Mass combat is handled through Warbands / Detachments battling each other. Keep zone combat in mind in the same way as you would regular combat.

Players implementing a good strategy, the fiction's situation or strong defenses can inflict Enhanced or Impaired damage when relevant.

Structures

Taking critical damage means the structure is breached or no longer provides protection. Repairing the damage requires one half of the domain requirements to construct it.
