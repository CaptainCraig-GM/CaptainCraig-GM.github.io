Fantasy Classes

Find an NPC that can teach you the ways. Takes a week of downtime and the silver cost for the level.

Fighter

Boon: Gain a +1 bonus to damage rolls for each level of fighter.

  1. Sunder Gear: You can break equipment in order to nullify an attack against you.
  2. Hilt Punch: You can change any melee damage type to bludgeoning.
  3. Gambits: Trade in 4 damage dealt to perform a maneuver with no save allowed.
  4. Second Attack: Attack two separate melee targets each round.

Cleric

Boon: Gain +1 hp for each level taken in cleric.
Bane: You don't suffer Spell Burn or Dooms, but instead suffer wrath:

  • Rolling Doubles means Dex Damage equal to roll.
  • Rolling Triples means Cha Damage equal to roll.

  1. Casting: Gain 1 MD.
    Turn Undead: Gain the Turn spell.
    Attire: You can benefit from MD invested in gear (Icon, Weapon, Shield).
    Kindly Warrior: You can only use blunt weapons. No piercing or Slashing.
  2. Holy Armour: Gain +1 armour, up to the maximum of 3.
    Divine Blessing: Gain the Heal spell.
  3. Guardian: Each round you may take damage in place of a close ally.
    Blessed: Gain an additional MD.
  4. Request: You can make an request of your deity. I'll roll the die of fate, God stuff on 1 or 2.

Magic User

Boon: Gain 1 language and 1 MD for each level taken in magic user.
First spell is free. You have to quest for or research more.

  1. Casting: You start with a spell book, and 1 random spell.
    Read Magic: You can decipher magic in scrolls, books and sigils.
    Sweet Duds: You can benefit from MD invested in gear (Staff, Robes, Pointy Hat).
  2. Crafty: You can spend crafting spells, scrolls and potions, if properly equipped.
  3. PolyTongue: You can read any written language.
  4. Enchanter: You can spend downtime enchanting items, if properly equipped.

Rogue

Boon: Gain 1 luck and 1 'skill' for each level taken in rogue..

  1. Backstab: Deal weapon +d8 bonus damage, directly off the targets Strength.
    Lucky: You can spend your luck pool on yourself.
    Skilled: Gain a -[level] bonus to your 'Skill Saves' (Skills are defined broadly: Sneaking, Climbing, Etc.)
  2. Arcane Adept: You can cast spells from Rods and Scrolls using the items MD.
  3. Climb Walls: Within reason.
  4. Luckier: You gain a second, equal pool of luck that only works on your friends.

Barbarian

Boon: Gain a pool of hp that are available while raging. +4 hp per level taken in barbarian.

  1. Rage: You can fly into a rage. While raging:
    • Strength Saves are rolled with advantage.
    • Each round after the first requires a Strength Save at a +[rounds raged] penalty to continue raging.
    • You deal +d10 bonus damage out, but take +d8 bonus damage in.
    Bikini Brawler: You need to MOVE ! No armour, but you can use a shield.
    Exhausting: Raging more than [level] times in a day leaves you deprived.
  2. Aware: Damage coming in is normal.
  3. Mad as hell...: You get a second attack each turn while raging.
  4. ...and Tough as Shit!: You can ignore critical damage while your rage lasts.
    If you hit 0 str, you get one last turn with +2d12 bonus damage and then you die.

Druid

Boon: Gain +1 MD for each level taken in druid. You don't use spellbooks, or gain MD from gear.

  1. Of the Wild:
    • You can heal lost Ability Points in the wilds as if sleeping in town with the 'Well Rested' and 'Well Fed' boons.
    • You categorically H A T E anything to do with civilization (Roads, Town, Steel, Speech, Books, Etc.)
    2. Vines: Gain use of the vines spell.
  2. Beast Tongue: You can roughly communicate with beasts. They don't always like you.
    Mists: Gain use of the mist spell.
  3. Beast Call: You can call [dice= x2: minions, x1 grunt] that attempt to help until death, or you release your MD invested.
    Tree Stride: Gain use of the tree stride spell.
  4. PolyMorph: Change into a bestial form. [dice= 1: grunt, 2: elite, 4: boss]
    Lasts while MD are invested, or death. Death as a beast means Death as a Druid.

Paladin

Boon: Gain +1 hp per level taken in Paladin.
Bane: Behaving in ways unbecoming of a good alignment looses any class benefit until restitution is made.
Doubles on MD means the effect is reversed.

  1. Valiant: [Level] times per combat, you can take a hit in place of a close ally. You take Impaired damage for these attacks.
  2. Compel: Each combat, you can compel a creature that understands you to Fight you, or Flee from you. Cha save at +[level] penalty
  3. Heal/Harm: You gain 2 MD which can be used to affect a creature's Strength. You must touch them. Appropriately.
  4. Glorious Vengeance: +Ed6 damage against Evil creatures.

Ranger

Boon: Each level taken in ranger grants a +1 bonus to damage rolled with your chosen weapon type.

  1. Favoured Weapon: Gain damage boon to chosen weapon attacks.
    Favoured Enemy:Deal +d8 bonus damage to chosen foes.
  2. Favoured Biome: Results of random encounters and the range in which they occur tend toward your favour. You are also less likely to get lost in your chosen biome.
  3. Animal Buddy: A woodland friend seeks you out (Minion). They will obey your commands to the best of their ability.
  4. Better All Round: Your buddy can now have the stats of a Grunt. You can choose a second enemy type and biome.

Living Portal

In your chest, where your heart would be, lies a portal that connects to another location.
Boon: Connect your inner portal to a different location for each level taken in living portal.

  1. Pocket Universe: You can link your portal to a extra dimensional space, the size of a treasure chest.
  2. Switcher: Spend a Turn concentrating on a location you have been, Your portal now connects there.
  3. Gaping: You can now stuff 2-slot or bulky items into your portal.   ( ͡° ͜ʖ ͡°)
  4. Fast Switcher: Swap connected locations at will.

Many Goblins

Maybe in a trench-coat, mostly spilled all over the room pell-mell, you are many goblins. Exactly how many can change from scene to scene.
Boon: Each level grants 2 more goblins (Str, this continues past 4th) Going over 16 Str splits, and those other 8 goblins go off to plague somebody else.

  1. Shared Totals: Str damage kills that many goblins in an inconsequential, background sort of way. Traps and area effects only effect them once. Instant-kill spells only kill one goblin at most.
    Critical damage means the goblins are knocked down like bowling pins and need help getting it together.
    Many goblins only have 5 inventory slots (they lose, smash, ruin, sell or eat anything else).
    To gain a benefit from gear, they need 10x of the object (shields, armour or fancy swords)
  2. Warlord: Each of your warlords gets a name, and a description. They can do something different than the other many goblins once each encounter.
  3. +1 Warlord: You can do two separate somethings.
    Use Thing: The many can now be given a magic item or special tool, and can collectively use it once per encounter.
  4. +1 Warlord: THREE SEPARATE SOMETHINGS !!
    New Thing: The many goblins now have six inventory slots, and once per encounter a new thing can be used.
    • roll d6 for this encounter's new thing
    • beehive slingshot, black smoking bombs, chain whacker, skelearms!, shackles, a giant snail, another goblin, a furious wolverine, a spike pit trap, chest with giant spring and bucket of oil, ratapult
